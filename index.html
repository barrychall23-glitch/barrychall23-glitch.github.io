The Process

How we fight back

STEP 01
Get the valuation report
Call your adjuster and demand the full Market Valuation Report. CCC, Mitchell, or Audatex — whatever they used. You are entitled to it. This document is the entire battlefield.
STEP 02
Identify the manipulation
We read every comparable vehicle — location, mileage, price, adjustments. Out-of-state comps, mileage outliers, Projected Sold Adjustments, wrong drivetrain. We find what they're hiding.
STEP 03
Build the counter-offer
We pull real local comps with VINs from your actual market, calculate the true replacement value, and draft a formal dispute letter citing your state's insurance statute by name.
STEP 04
Send it in writing
Everything goes in email. Every demand. Every objection. Every escalation. Phone calls leave no trail. A documented paper trail is what forces insurance companies to revise their numbers.
Insurance Tactics

What they do to pay you less

Out-of-State Comparables

They pull vehicles from 60–80 miles away in different states where prices are lower, ignoring your local market entirely.

SEEN IN CASE 001 · GEICO

High-Mileage Outliers

One $4,900 vehicle with 163,847 miles pulls the weighted average down for everyone. They count it the same as a fair comp.

SEEN IN CASE 001 · GEICO

Projected Sold Adjustments

They deduct $550–$620 from every listing price, assuming you'd negotiate down. Toyota Corollas sell at asking price. This is fiction.

SEEN IN CASE 002 · ARBELLA

Selective Comp Acceptance

You submit 3 comps at $15,988, $17,794, and $17,998. They accept only the cheapest one — no explanation for the others.

SEEN IN CASE 002 · ARBELLA

Storage Fee Pressure

"Sign now or storage fees get deducted." They use your car sitting in a lot as a countdown clock to force a bad settlement.

SEEN IN CASE 002 · ARBELLA

Wrong Drivetrain Comps

RWD vehicles compared against your AWD vehicle in a winter state. Different value. Different demand. Same adjustment. Wrong.
