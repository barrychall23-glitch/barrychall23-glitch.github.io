The Process

How we fight back

1
Get the valuation report
Call your adjuster and demand the full Market Valuation Report — CCC ONE, Mitchell WorkCenter, or Audatex. You are legally entitled to it. This document is the entire battlefield and most people never even ask for it.
2
Identify what they're hiding
We read every comparable vehicle — location, mileage, list price, adjustments, drivetrain. Out-of-state comps, mileage outliers, Projected Sold Adjustments, wrong vehicle types. We find the manipulation line by line.
3
Build the counter-offer
We pull real local dealer listings with VINs from your actual market, calculate the true replacement value, and draft a formal dispute letter that cites your state's insurance law by name — the same way we did in both cases.
4
Send everything in writing
Every demand, every objection, every escalation request goes in email. Phone calls leave no trail. A documented paper trail is what forces insurance companies to revise their number — or face a regulatory complaint.
Know Their Playbook

6 ways they pay you less

📍

Out-of-State Comparables

They pull vehicles from 60–80 miles away in different states where prices run lower — ignoring your actual local replacement market.

→ SEEN IN CASE 001 · GEICO
📉

High-Mileage Outliers

One $4,900 vehicle at 163,847 miles pulls the entire weighted average down. It counts the same as a legitimate comparable.

→ SEEN IN CASE 001 · GEICO
✂️

Projected Sold Adjustments

They assume you'd negotiate $550–$620 off every listing price. This is a fictional estimate applied as hard math — not real transaction data.

→ SEEN IN CASE 002 · ARBELLA
🎯

Selective Comp Acceptance

You submit comps at $15,988, $17,794, and $17,998. They accept only the cheapest and exclude the rest with no written explanation.

→ SEEN IN CASE 002 · ARBELLA
⏱️

Storage Fee Pressure

"Sign now or storage fees get deducted from your settlement." They use your car sitting in a lot as a countdown clock to force a bad deal.

→ SEEN IN CASE 002 · ARBELLA
🚗

Wrong Drivetrain Comps

RWD vehicles compared against your AWD in a New England winter market. Completely different demand and value — treated identically.

→ SEEN IN CASE 001 · GEICO
